// The Two-Pillar System

Core Variables Behind the Strategy

The framework is built on two mandatory inputs: price-action structure and option-chain confirmation. A trade is considered valid only when both inputs point in the same direction.

PILLAR 01 — PRICE ACTION
What the Chart Shows
The chart-analysis layer uses TradingView with SMC (Smart Money Concepts) overlays. The primary markings generally include:

• Order Blocks (OBs) — green (bullish) and red (bearish) shaded rectangles where institutional activity happened. Seen clearly in the Aug 26 Nifty 1hr chart — green OB between 24,733–24,835, red OB below. Price entered green zone → shot up.

• Fibonacci levels — 0.236, 0.382, 0.5, 0.618, 0.786. From the Dec 17 daily chart, Nifty was at exactly 0.5 fib (24,775) and failed to close above it → bearish trade.

• Trendlines — drawn on 5m, 15m, and 1hr charts. BankNifty Sep 23 chart shows a descending triangle trendline with price consolidating → anticipating either breakout or breakdown.

• Previous day H/L — a frequently used trigger line. A breakout is treated as valid only when price crosses and sustains above or below the relevant level.
PILLAR 02 — OI DATA (Sensibull/OI Tracker App)
What the Market Tells
The market-data layer uses option-chain and open-interest tracking tools. The key observable fields include:

• Call OI Change vs Put OI Change in Crores — the two numbers at top of every OI screenshot. When Put OI Change >> Call OI Change in green = PUT buyers dominant = bullish.

• Strike-wise OI bars — green = put OI increase, red = call OI increase. A cluster of tall red bars above current price = heavy resistance (call writers defending). Tall green bars below = support (put writers defending).

• PCR (Put-Call Ratio) — shown on every OI screenshot. PCR 0.48 on Oct 17 → bearish (2x call sellers). PCR 1.32 on Dec 3 → bullish (more put sellers). PCR 1.08 on Nov 25 with rally = confirmed.

• INDIA VIX — shown alongside PCR. VIX 14.77 on Dec 17 with market falling = premiums cheap but decay high.
The central principle is simple: price action provides the structural setup, while data confirms whether market participation supports the trade. — Method summary

The 3 Core Trading Setups

The strategy can be organized into three repeatable setups. Each setup links market structure, OI confirmation, entry trigger, strike selection, target logic, and stop-loss definition.

Setup A: The Day High Breakout
5M / 15M CHART · CE TRADE · ~60% of all trades
Seen in Aug 26, Sep 23, Nov 25, Apr 13 charts
Pre-condition Market opens flat or slightly red. First 30–45 mins establishes a clear day high.
OI filter Buyer OI > Seller OI by ≥1 Cr. PCR ≥ 0.9.
Entry trigger Day high crossed AND 5-min candle closes above it (not just wick). Price holds for 1 more candle.
Strike selection ATM CE or 1 strike OTM CE. Not deep OTM unless hero-zero intent.
Target T1: 50–70% premium gain. T2: 80–120% gain. (Book T1 first)
Stop Loss Close back below day high on 5-min basis. Or 15% premium loss.
Why it works Day high is where call sellers set up. When it breaks, they panic-cover → forced short-covering momentum. OI shift shows the cover starting.
Setup B: The OB Bounce
15M / 1H CHART · CE or PE · ~25% of trades
Seen in Aug 26 (1hr OB bounce), Apr 13 2026 (1m OB after gap down)
Pre-condition Identify green Order Block (bullish OB) on 15m or 1hr where prior big momentum candle originated.
OI filter As price enters OB zone: Put OI increasing (buyers defending). Call OI stable or falling.
Entry trigger Price touches OB. A rejection candle forms (hammer, doji, bullish engulfing). Enter on next candle open.
Strike selection ATM CE. Premium should be 80–200rs range for meaningful R:R.
Target Previous swing high. Typically 1.5x to 2x premium.
Stop Loss Candle close BELOW OB zone (OB violated). Hard exit.
Aug 26 example Nifty entered 24,733–24,835 OB on 1hr. Bounced → shot to 25,040. "NIFTY TGT HIT."
Setup C: VCP + Trendline Break
5M / 15M CHART · CE or PE · ~15% of trades
Seen in Sep 23 BNifty descending triangle chart, Dec 4 Nifty 15m
Pattern Price makes lower highs but lows are contracting (VCP) or a descending trendline forms after a spike.
OI filter OI data should match break direction: breaking up = buyer OI rising. Breaking down = seller OI rising.
Entry trigger Trendline breaks. Wait for a RETEST of the broken trendline (price comes back). Enter on rejection from the retest.
Strike selection Slightly OTM in break direction. This trade has high success rate but is slower.
Target Measured move = height of pattern projected from breakout point.
Stop Loss Break back through trendline on 5-min close basis.
Sep 23 example BNifty descending triangle on 5m. Arrows drawn: up scenario +281pts, down scenario −180pts. Market chose up → 5,623 range in 3 bars.
// Exact Step-by-Step

The Entry Execution System

This section describes the execution sequence in a textbook format, starting from pre-market bias formation and ending with trade activation and hedging decisions.

1
Pre-Market (8:00–9:10 AM) — The Gift Nifty Read
Check Gift Nifty for the likely opening gap direction. A large positive or negative indication sets the initial directional bias. Cross-check the signal with Dow Jones futures or other global-market cues.

Key question: Is the gap a runaway gap or will it fill? If Gift Nifty >200 points up but FII had been short all week → gap up but sell-on-rise setup.
Gift Nifty Dow Futures FII Data from prior day
2
First 15 Minutes (9:15–9:30 AM) — DO NOT TRADE
Avoid entering during the first 15 minutes. This opening window often contains noise, gap adjustments, and false moves. The key observation is whether price opens above, below, or inside the previous day's range, and whether it holds or rejects that level.

The rule is to avoid premature trades and wait for a confirmed breakout or breakdown with candle-close validation.
NO TRADE ZONE Gap fill or extend? Watch, not act
3
OI Check at 9:30–10:00 AM — The Go/No-Go Decision
Open the OI tracker and compare Call OI change vs Put OI change. This reveals whether call writers, put writers, call buyers, or put buyers are dominating the current session.

If OI direction matches chart direction → proceed. If they contradict → no trade.
Buyer OI dominant → CE setup Seller OI dominant → PE setup Equal OI → Butterfly only
4
Level Identification — Mark Before Entry
Mark on chart: Day High, Previous Day High/Low, nearby OB zones, Fib levels. These are the exact levels shown in every chart photo. The red line on Dec 4 chart = resistance. The green shaded OB = demand zone. These are non-negotiable — no entry without knowing the levels.

Document the levels before entry, for example: R1: 22732, R2: 23029, S1: 22193, S2: 21843. The purpose is to avoid emotional decisions after price starts moving.
Day High/Low OB Zones (SMC) Fib 0.5 / 0.618 Gap fills
5
Trigger Activation — The Precise Entry
Wait for setup to activate. The exact trigger: 5-minute candle closes above/below the level (not just touches it). Then check OI one more time — is it still aligned? If yes, enter immediately.

From the Apr 13 chart: Price gapped down, consolidated in a green OB zone (23,634–23,700), then broke above day high at 14:30. Trade activated. CE moved 135 points (0.57%) in the next hour.

From the Jan 27 position screenshot: Entered NIFTY 30 Jan 22900 CE @ ₹202 average. Exited at ₹246.50. Profit: ₹1,112.50 on 25 qty.
5-min candle close above level OI still aligned → enter Wick only → wait
6
Hedge Setup — Mandatory for Positional Trades
For intraday scalps: no hedge needed. For BTST or multi-day: always hedge. The BankNifty butterfly position (Dec 18 screenshot) shows exactly this — 4 legs: Buy 52400 CE (long), Sell 52900 CE (short, the body), Sell 53100 CE (short), Buy 53400 CE (long wing). This defines max risk to ~₹1,000 while keeping ₹9,000+ upside.

Simple hedge for directional trade: "Buy CE + sell OTM CE." Example structure: "Buy 24850 CE @94, TGT 150, SL 65 — Risk 2500."
Bull call spread Bear put spread Butterfly (sideways)
// When to Book, When to Hold

The Exit System

Exit management is the most important part of the framework. The rules below define when to book, trail, or invalidate a trade.

BOOK IMMEDIATELY WHEN

✅ T1 is hit — book 50–70% of position. Let rest run with trail SL.

✅ Reversal candle appears at target zone — don't wait for exact number.

✅ OI data flips against you mid-trade — if CE trade and sellers suddenly add 2Cr OI, exit regardless of P&L.

✅ Market hits any institutional resistance or OB on higher TF — book partial.

✅ Position is green 30–50% — on expiry day, partial or full booking is preferred because theta decay and intraday reversals can quickly reduce option premium.

✅ Profit from hero-zero position hits 100% — book. Do not hold for 500%.
EXIT MANDATORY (SL HIT)

❌ 5-min candle closes below your entry level — setup invalidated.

❌ 15% premium loss — hard stop, no exceptions.

❌ OB / Order Block violated on 15m close — immediate exit. If the order block fails to provide support or resistance, the setup is invalid.

❌ Day Low breaks when you're in a CE trade — exit all CE positions. The market has shown you its hand.

❌ Opposite OI builds suddenly (>2 Cr in 10 mins) — exit even if still slightly green.

❌ VIX spikes hard while in a sideways trade — butterfly/condor positions exit immediately.
Options require disciplined profit booking because premium can decay or reverse quickly. A predefined target is preferred over chasing an uncertain larger move. — Exit discipline principle
THE TRAILING SL SYSTEM (observed from charts)

After T1 is hit, the trailing stop-loss method can be applied as follows:

Step 1: Book 50–60% at T1. Move SL to breakeven (entry price).
Step 2: As price moves toward T2, trail SL to T1 level.
Step 3: If price consolidates for 2+ candles, exit remaining position.
Step 4: Once the projected target is achieved, close the remaining position even if further movement is possible. The objective is rule-based execution, not maximum-point capture.

Example case: after price moved meaningfully beyond the stop-loss reference and reached the second target zone, the remaining position was closed instead of being held for an uncertain extended move.
// Capital Management from Actual Trades

Position Sizing Rules

The sizing model below converts the strategy into practical capital allocation rules across standard trades, high-risk trades, and defined-risk structures.

TYPE A — CORE TRADE (70% of capital)
Standard Setup Trade
For core setups, the observed sizing range is approximately 25–75 quantity depending on index, premium, and available capital.

Jan 27 screenshot: 25 qty NIFTY 22900 CE @ ₹202 = ₹5,050 deployed. Exited ₹246.50 = ₹1,112 profit (22% return).

Feb 3 screenshot: 75 qty NIFTY 23250 CE @ ₹176 = ₹13,230 deployed. Exited ₹211 = ₹2,602 profit (19.7% return).

Rule: Max deployment per trade = 1–3% of total FnO capital. So if your FnO capital is ₹5 lakh, max one trade = ₹5,000–15,000 premium.
TYPE B — HERO-ZERO (max 10% of capital)
High Risk / High Reward
Low premium OTM options (₹5–20 each). Only 1 lot. Only from profits already earned that day.

"Lupin 2180 CE @ 13.50" — 1 lot. If goes to 0, max loss = ₹1,350. If goes 5x = ₹6,750.

Low-premium OTM options should be classified as speculative hero-zero trades, not as core strategy trades.

Rule: Only enter if: (a) there's a catalyst, (b) premium is ≤₹20, (c) money is from day's profits, not capital.
TYPE C — BUTTERFLY / SELLING (25% of capital)
Sideways Market Income
BankNifty butterfly screenshot: 60 qty at 52400 CE (buy), 90 at 52900 CE (sell), 15 at 53100 CE (sell), 45 at 53400 CE (buy).

Net debit is designed to remain small, with defined risk and limited directional exposure. Profit potential depends on expiry settlement relative to the strategy body.

Used when VIX is low (below 13) and PCR is neutral (0.8–1.2).

Rule: Complex multi-leg strategies may require higher margin, often around ₹1–1.5 lakh or more depending on broker, strikes, and volatility. They are not ideal for very small accounts.

Chart Pattern Recognition Guide

These chart patterns are commonly used in the framework. They are ranked by practical relevance and frequency of occurrence in directional FnO analysis.

📐 Order Block (OB) Bounce Most Used
What it looks like: A rectangular zone (green or red) drawn around a cluster of candles that preceded a strong move. On SMC indicator, auto-plotted.

From Aug 26 chart: Green OB = 24,733–24,835 zone (red shaded = bearish zone below, green = bullish above). Price dipped into green OB at 22-Aug, formed base, then shot to 25,040 on 26-Aug. "NIFTY TGT HIT."

Entry rule: Only enter when price is INSIDE the OB and shows a rejection candle. Never chase price above OB.

Timeframe: 1hr for swing, 15m for intraday Success rate: High (OB + OI aligned)
📉 M Pattern / Double Top (Bearish)
What it looks like: Two peaks at nearly equal heights with a valley in between. Neckline = the low between peaks.

Research note: "M pattern breakdown then retest with OB rejection" — Dec 2025 chart. "30 mins M pattern breakdown retest" — Dec 16.

Entry rule: Wait for neckline break AND retest. If price comes back to neckline and is rejected (with red OI) → enter PE. Target = depth of M projected below neckline.

Use for: PE trades, bearish swings Confirmation: OI shift bearish on retest
📊 VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern)
What it looks like: Price swings get progressively narrower. Each high is lower, each low is higher, but the range CONTRACTS. Like a coiling spring.

Research note: "Yesterday nifty did a VCP on 5 mins" (Sep 2024). "Nifty hourly chart bana raha he bearish VCP" (Feb 2026).

Entry rule: Buy/Sell the breakout of the last contraction level. The tighter the VCP, the more explosive the breakout. OI must confirm direction.

Works in: Both bullish and bearish VCPs
📈 Flag / Bull Flag (Blasting Stocks)
What it looks like: A sharp vertical move (pole) followed by a tight consolidation that moves slightly against the trend (the flag).

Research note: "flag pattern ka tgt he 377" — stock at 303, breakout from ~240. Pole height ~63 points → target = 303 + 63 = ~366–377. Exact target hit.

Entry rule: Enter on flag breakout. Do NOT enter in middle of flag. SL = bottom of flag. Target = pole height projected from breakout.

Best for: Stock FnO (stock FnO watchlist) Timeframe: 15m or daily for stock setups
🔵 FVG (Fair Value Gap)
What it looks like: A price gap between three candles where candle 1's range doesn't overlap with candle 3's range. Creates a void that price tends to fill.

Research note: FVG observations should be treated as contextual price zones first. A trade is taken only when price reaction, candle structure, and OI confirmation align near the FVG.

Note: FVG is primarily used as context and as a potential decision zone, not always as a direct entry signal. It often acts as a price-magnet area before continuation or reversal.

FVG = price magnet / decision zone
🔻 Descending Trendline Resistance (Bearish Bias)
What it looks like: Lower highs forming a downward-sloping line. Every time price touches this line, it gets rejected.

Research note: ITC chart (Apr 17) shows descending trendline clearly drawn in red. Price approached 303.30 (trendline) → showed hammer → shot up to 307.60. This represented a long-side setup.

Flip scenario: When descending trendline breaks AND retests and holds → major bullish setup. A break-and-retest above the descending trendline confirms a potential long-side continuation.

Break + retest = highest conviction entry

// The Non-Negotiable Rules

The 10 Laws of This Strategy

Break any of these and the strategy stops working. These rules summarize the non-negotiable controls required for consistent execution.

📊
Law 1: OI Must Confirm Direction Never enter a CE trade when Call OI is adding heavily. Never enter PE when Put OI is adding heavily. They are the enemy. Wait until OI aligns.
⏱️
Law 2: Candle Must CLOSE — Not Touch A wick touching a level means nothing. Only a 5-minute candle CLOSE above/below the level counts as confirmation. Confirmation requires a completed 5-minute candle close.
🚫
Law 3: No Trade Without a Setup Name Day High Breakout. OB Bounce. VCP Break. Trendline retest. If the pattern cannot be clearly named, the trade should be avoided.
💸
Law 4: Book Fast. Hold Kills. Options decay. Take partial profit at T1 and trail the remaining quantity. Holding without a predefined exit can quickly reduce premium value.
🛡️
Law 5: Always Hedge Positional Trades For BTST or multi-day: buy a protective option in opposite direction. The butterfly screenshot shows this perfectly. Naked overnight = unacceptable risk.
📉
Law 6: Respect VIX for Strategy Choice VIX >16 = buy options (premiums pay well). VIX <12 = sell options / butterfly (decay wins). This single filter prevents most CE-decay losses.
🧠
Law 7: Screen Overrides Bias Trade observable market structure rather than personal bias. When the screen invalidates the original view, exit or stand aside.
🎯
Law 8: FII Futures Are the Compass FII adding shorts in futures often supports a sell-on-rise environment. FII short-covering can support sharp rallies. Review institutional futures data regularly.
⚡
Law 9: Expiry Is for Observation Only On expiry day, premiums can move erratically. Prefer observation or very small defined-risk speculative trades. Expiry-day manipulation and theta decay risk are elevated.
📐
Law 10: Size for Pain Tolerance If the maximum planned loss creates emotional pressure, position size is too large. FnO capital should be risk capital only.
Consistent learning in FnO comes from structured review, disciplined risk control, and repeated observation. Analysis should precede every trade decision. — Research summary
